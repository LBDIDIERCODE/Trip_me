France Poland Italy

Eiffel Tower

: Known as the "City of Light," Paris is a world-renowned destination that attracts millions of visitors each year. The city offers iconic landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Champs-Élysées. Visitors can also explore charming neighborhoods like Montmartre, enjoy world-class cuisine, and experience the vibrant art and culture scene.p

Provence

: Located in the southeastern part of France, Provence is famous for its picturesque landscapes, charming villages, and rich history. Visitors can explore the lavender fields in Valensole, visit the vibrant city of Aix-en-Provence, discover the stunning Gorges du Verdon, and experience the colorful markets and historic sites in Avignon.

French Riviera

Also known as the Côte d'Azur, the French Riviera offers glamorous coastal towns, beautiful beaches, and a luxurious lifestyle. Destinations like Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez attract visitors with their stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and renowned festivals like the Cannes Film Festival. The region also offers opportunities to explore charming hilltop villages, such as Eze and Saint-Paul-de-Vence.

Warsaw

Poland's capital and largest city, Warsaw, offers a mix of historic landmarks and modern attractions. The Old Town of Warsaw, also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, has been beautifully restored after World War II and is a must-visit. Visitors can explore the Royal Castle, stroll along the vibrant Nowy Świat street, and visit the poignant Warsaw Uprising Museum. Warsaw also has a thriving cultural scene with numerous museums, theaters, and art galleries.

Zakopane and the Tatra Mountains

Located in the southern part of Poland, Zakopane is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. It serves as a gateway to the stunning Tatra Mountains, which offer scenic hiking trails, breathtaking landscapes, and opportunities for skiing in the winter. Zakopane itself is known for its distinctive wooden architecture, vibrant markets, and the unique culture of the highlanders (Górale).

Kraków

Located in southern Poland, Kraków is a historic and cultural gem. The city's Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is home to the magnificent Wawel Castle, the Main Market Square, and the medieval St. Mary's Basilica. Visitors can also explore the historic Jewish Quarter (Kazimierz), visit the fascinating Schindler's Factory Museum, and take a day trip to the nearby Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial and Museum.

Rome, Italty

As the capital city and a living museum itself, Rome is a must-visit destination for history buffs and art enthusiasts. The city is home to iconic landmarks such as the Colosseum, Roman Forum, and Pantheon. Visitors can explore Vatican City, the smallest independent state in the world, where they can marvel at St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums, including the famous Sistine Chapel. Rome also offers picturesque piazzas, charming neighborhoods like Trastevere, and world-class cuisine.

Florence

Located in the heart of Tuscany, Florence is renowned for its artistic heritage and architectural masterpieces. The city is home to iconic sites such as the Florence Cathedral (Duomo), the Uffizi Gallery, and the Ponte Vecchio. Visitors can explore the historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and admire works of art by famous Renaissance artists like Michelangelo and Botticelli. Florence is also a gateway to the beautiful Tuscan countryside, with its rolling hills, vineyards, and charming towns like Siena and San Gimignano.

Venice

Known as the "Floating City," Venice is a unique and romantic destination built on a network of canals. Visitors can explore the city's iconic landmarks, including St. Mark's Square, St. Mark's Basilica, and the Doge's Palace. Taking a gondola ride through the canals is a quintessential Venice experience. Visitors can also immerse themselves in the city's rich culture by visiting the Peggy Guggenheim Collection or attending an opera performance at La Fenice. Venice is famous for its annual Carnival and the Venice Biennale, an international art exhibition.
